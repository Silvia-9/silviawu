The End of the Social Network

Posted: 1st September 2024

Social media is no longer very social.
An article from The Economist highlights a significant decline in the way Americans document their lives online, dropping from 40% to 28% since 2020. The piece also raises concerns about the potential shutdown of messaging platforms like WhatsApp and Telegram (Wilson, 2024). This topic takes me back to my college years when I first started chronicling my life online. At that time, blogging, sharing images, and voicing opinions were incredibly popular. However, my journey took a negative turn when my blog was compromised, leading to the loss of all my photographs. This unfortunate event prompted me to transition to private posts, where I now preserve my memories for personal contemplation. Looking forward, I anticipate that more individuals will prefer using images and videos to document their daily lives, choosing visual storytelling over traditional written accounts.
