Posted: 24th September 2024

Last week, I had the chance to take part in a three-day online WomenHack AI bootcamp organized by Siemens. The majority of the participants were employees based in Germany. Prior to the bootcamp, I spent nearly two weeks trying to figure out how to access their Microsoft Teams platform. Just when I was about to give up, I finally succeeded in joining the group. Once the bootcamp commenced, I was assigned a task that differed from the one I had selected during registration.

However, I quickly adjusted to the new assignment and grasped what was required. It turned out to be a process quite similar to what I had experienced in a previous summer data innovation bootcamp, albeit with different tasks and a focus on Siemens' power system consulting. Our group's objective was to develop a chatbot to assist with daily operations. Throughout this bootcamp, I learned how to create a minimum viable product (MVP) independently, which was a refreshing experience, and I discovered new ways to present my pitch. Additionally, I found solutions to challenges from the earlier bootcamp and, most importantly, I gained valuable experience in collaborating with teammates from various countries.

The End of the Social Network

Posted: 1st September 2024

Social media is no longer very social.
An article from The Economist highlights a significant decline in the way Americans document their lives online, dropping from 40% to 28% since 2020. The piece also raises concerns about the potential shutdown of messaging platforms like WhatsApp and Telegram (Wilson, 2024). This topic takes me back to my college years when I first started chronicling my life online. At that time, blogging, sharing images, and voicing opinions were incredibly popular. However, my journey took a negative turn when my blog was compromised, leading to the loss of all my photographs. This unfortunate event prompted me to transition to private posts, where I now preserve my memories for personal contemplation. Looking forward, I anticipate that more individuals will prefer using images and videos to document their daily lives, choosing visual storytelling over traditional written accounts.
